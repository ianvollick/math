Algebraic Manipulation Exercises 1

Please note that in the following examples the little dots mean "multiply". So this \(3 \cdot 2\) means the same as \(3 \times 2\) (which equals \(6\), of course).

Example \(1\). Solve for \(y\). $$\begin{align} \frac{y}{3} & = 2\\ \frac{y}{3}\cdot 3 & = 2 \cdot 3 & \text{(Multiply by \(3\))} \\ y & = 6 & \text{(Simplify)} \end{align}$$ Example \(2\). Solve for \(y\). $$\begin{align} \frac{4y}{5} & = 8 \\ \frac{4y}{5} \cdot 5 & = 8 \cdot 5 & \text{(Multiply by \(5\))} \\ 4y & = 40 & \text{(Simplify)}\\ 4y \cdot \frac{1}{4} & = 40 \cdot \frac{1}{4} & \text{(Divide by \(4\))} \\ \frac{4y}{4} & = \frac{40}{4} & \text{(Simplify)} \\ y & = 10 & \text{(Simplify)} \end{align}$$

Algebraic Manipulation Exercises 2

\(1.\) Lisa is at a well. If Lisa pulls the rope on a pully \(5m\), the bucket rises \(2m\). How far will Lisa have to pull to lift the bucket \(3m\)?
\(2.\) A hose pours \(30L\) every \(4min\). How long will it take to fill a \(1200L\) pool?
\(3.\) Jorge earns \($35.00\) every hour at work. If he works for \(12.5\) hours, how much money will he have earned?
\(4.\) Garfield the cat coughs up \(4\) hairballs a week. How many hairballs will he cough up in \(120.5\) weeks?
